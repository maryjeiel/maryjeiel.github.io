I keep finding myself in these arguments about admitting things and owning up to mistakes. There is this strange part where you are telling the truth but the other person just does not believe you. It feels like being an innocent person in a courtroom where everyone wants you to confess to something you did not even do.

Or maybe you do not fully understand the situation yet, so you end up admitting things you do not even understand. That is still wrong.

I am learning that you need to understand the problem first before trying to fix it. Not to look good, but because real clarity comes from honesty. From owning the parts where you actually messed up, and not forcing responsibility just to end the tension.

According to Harvard’s history, Veritas has been part of its identity since 1643. But truth is not just about not lying. It is about actively seeking what is real, even when it is uncomfortable. Especially when it makes you look bad. Especially when staying quiet or softening things would be easier.

Veritas, to me, is starting to feel like a way of life. Avoiding truth is one of the worst things a person can do.

It is better to be good than to look good. Do not perform goodness. Just be it.
