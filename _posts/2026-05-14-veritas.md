On truth, admission, and the difference between the two.


I kept having these arguments about admitting things and owning up to mistakes. There's this part where you're telling the truth but the other person just doesn't believe you. It's like being an innocent person in a courtroom where everyone wants you to confess to something you didn't even do. Or maybe you don't fully understand the situation yet, so you end up admitting to things you don't even understand, which is just as wrong.

You need to understand the problem first, then come up with a solution. Not to look good, but because looking good actually comes from telling the truth and owning the parts where you genuinely messed up.

Truth isn't just about not lying. It's about actively seeking truth, even when it's uncomfortable.

According to Harvard's history, Veritas has been a central part of the university's identity since 1643. But truth isn't just about not lying. It's about actively seeking truth, even when it's uncomfortable. Even when the truth makes you look bad. Even when staying quiet or sugarcoating things would be so much easier.

[Harvard University, History of Shields](https://www.harvard.edu/about/history/shields/)

Veritas is a way of life. Avoiding truth is one of the worst things a person can do. That's why it's easier to be good than to look good. Don't perform goodness. Just be it.
