date: 2026-04-02

There is a certain kind of tension that comes with growth. The pull between letting go and holding on, not out of weakness, but out of awareness. That is exactly where I am right now.

I am learning to release the dreams that once showed me how beautiful this world could be. Not because they no longer matter, but because I have realized that some dreams are not endings. They are doors. And sometimes the most courageous thing you can do is walk through one without knowing what waits on the other side.

What this season of my life gave me was something I did not expect: proof that I am still capable of feeling deeply.

After everything I have been through, the trauma, the anxiety, the quiet weight of stress carried every day, I thought maybe I had closed off that part of myself for good. But I had not.

Looking back now, I find myself strangely grateful for those difficult seasons. The trauma sharpened my discernment. Anxiety taught me endurance. Depression brought me low enough to understand what it truly means to appreciate being alive. Even the stress helped shape the version of me standing here today.

I am still young. Still learning and still becoming.

I follow the Harvard Plate. I do yoga. I exercise. I intentionally challenge myself to learn things that frustrate me, because discomfort has always been where I grow the most.

There is still so much of this world I have not seen, and so much about myself I have yet to understand. And I intend to find out.

He was the reason I started looking. But I am the reason I will keep going.
