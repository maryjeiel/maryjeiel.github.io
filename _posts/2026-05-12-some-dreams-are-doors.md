title: "Some Dreams Are Doors"
date: 2026-05-12

There is a certain kind of tension that comes with growth. The pull between letting go and holding on, not out of weakness, but out of awareness. That is exactly where I am right now.

I am choosing to release the dreams that once showed me how beautiful this world could be. Not because they no longer matter, but because I have learned that some dreams are not endings. They are doors. And sometimes the most courageous thing you can do is walk through one even when you are not sure what is on the other side.

What this season of my life gave me is something I did not expect: proof that I am still capable of feeling deeply. After everything I have been through, the trauma, the anxiety, the depression, the weight of stress carried quietly every single day, I thought maybe I had closed off that part of myself for good. But I had not.

And looking back now, I am grateful for every single one of those hard things. The trauma sharpened my discernment. The anxiety, as unbearable as it was, taught me how to fight. The depression that once brought me to my lowest point is the very reason I now understand what it means to be truly grateful for being alive. The stress built the version of me standing here today.

I am still young. I am learning, moving, growing. I follow the Harvard Plate, I do yoga, I exercise, and I push myself to learn things that frustrate me on purpose. Because discomfort is where I have always grown the most.

There is still so much of this world I have not seen, so much I do not yet understand about myself. And I intend to find out.

He was the reason I started looking. But I am the reason I will keep going.
